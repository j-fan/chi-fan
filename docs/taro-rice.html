Taro fried rice

By Tammy Dang
Taro rice is a recipe that’s been passed down our family for 4 generations (great grandma, grandma, mum and me). We’re a Teochew family who fled to Vietnam during times of political unrest before going to Australia. So, the recipe has some Vietnamese touches as well. There isn’t a “true recipe” for this, kind of like fried rice. Instead, you use what ingredients you have to make it yummy and to your liking.
Taro rice first came into our family when there was political unrest and war. During those times, there was not enough rice to go around big Chinese families, so my great grandma and grandma would add some taro to make it more filling. Since then, there have been many iterations of this recipe within our family. This is my mums…

Directions

Preparing the ingredients:


  1. Wash rice at least 2 times, then add water to it (Mum’s note: To do this, place your palm flat on the rice and make sure the water reaches your the beginning of your knuckles. That is the perfect amount of water). Set the rice aside for now.
  2. Start peeling the skin of the taro then cut it into small wedges. (Mum’s note: Remember to wear gloves because my mum says that the skin will make your hands itchy).
  3. Salt the rice with a teaspoon of salt then add your taro wedges into the rice.
  4. Now, give it a little more salt and add some water to wet the taro a little bit.
  5. Set it in the rice cooker and cook it.
  6. Chop the spring onions into small pieces.
  7. Pull the mint off their stems and chop it into small pieces.
  8. Cut up your mushrooms of choice into small pieces.
  9. Cut the meats of choice (Here it is vietnamese bologna (Chả lụa), dried shrimp, and ham) into small pieces.

Stir frying the rice:


  1. When the rice is finished cooking, give it a good mix.
  2. Now prepare a wok or a pan with some oil.
  3. When it’s hot, throw in all of the chosen meats and vegetables.
  4. Add some chilli powder, teriyaki sauce, oyster sauce, soy sauce and black peppers into the pan.
  5. Stir fry on medium-high heat for a few minutes before putting the cooked taro and rice into the pan.
  6. Add extra seasoning to your taste (Mum’s note: always taste your food while you’re cooking and season to your taste!).
  7. Stir fry everything together! You can leave on the heat for longer if you want to build a crunchy rice crust.
  8. Your taro rice is ready!
